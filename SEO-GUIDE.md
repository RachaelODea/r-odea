## How easy is it for people to find you today?

**Honest answer:** Right now, it is **easy** for people who already know your name (e.g. they search "R-ODEA" or "r-odea.com"). It is **hard** for people searching generic terms like "luxury PR agency London" or "Asia market entry consultancy" — because many established agencies compete for those searches.

### What you already have going for you

- Clear positioning: strategic communications, brand partnerships, cultural positioning, Asia expansion
- Geographic hubs: London, Hong Kong, Melbourne
- Solid service and sector pages
- Basic SEO in place: page titles and meta descriptions on each page

### What is holding SEO back

| Gap | Why it matters |
|-----|----------------|
| Very little indexable text | Google ranks pages with useful, specific content — not mainly visuals and slogans |
| Homepage headline is poetic, not descriptive | "We will change the way you look at things" does not tell Google what you do |
| No sitemap.xml or robots.txt | Harder for search engines to discover and crawl all pages |
| No Open Graph / social meta tags | Weak previews when links are shared on LinkedIn, Instagram, etc. |
| Thin contact page description | "Contact R-ODEA" is too vague |
| Some images have empty alt text | Missed keyword and accessibility signals |
| No case studies, blog, or press page | Few chances to rank for long-tail searches |
| Intro "Enter" screen | Extra step before content; can hurt crawlability and first impression |

### Who will find you easily today

- People searching **"R-ODEA"** or **"r-odea.com"** (branded search)
- People you send the link to directly
- Referrals from LinkedIn, email, mother agents, and clients

### Who will not find you easily yet

- People searching generic terms like "luxury brand consultancy Asia" or "talent PR Hong Kong" — unless you build content and authority around those topics over time.

## Can keywords be hidden?

**Short answer: No — and you should not try.**

### What does NOT work (and can hurt you)

- White text on white background
- Keywords in hidden CSS (`display: none`) or tiny font
- Long lists of keywords in the footer
- The old `<meta name="keywords">` tag — Google has ignored it for years

Google treats these as **spam** and may **lower your rankings** or remove pages from results.

### What DOES work

Keywords should appear in normal, visible places:

1. **Meta description** — the short summary Google may show under your link
2. **Page title** — the most important on-page signal
3. **Headings (H1, H2)** — descriptive, not only poetic
4. **Body copy** — your About page is a good model
5. **Image alt text** — describe the image and context
6. **Structured data** — Organization schema with name, locations, services
7. **Sitemap + Google Search Console** — submit your site so Google knows what to index

**Bottom line:** Hidden keywords do not help. Clear, useful content that matches what people search for does.

## Step-by-step action plan

Do these steps in order. Steps 1–4 need no coding. Steps 5–8 are website changes.

---

### Part 1: Set up the basics (do this first)

#### Step 1: Put your site on Google's radar

1. Go to [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console)
2. Sign in with a Google account
3. Click **Add property**
4. Enter your website address (e.g. `https://r-odea.com`)
5. Follow Google's steps to prove you own the site (usually adding a small file to your site, or a DNS record)
6. Once verified, go to **Sitemaps** and submit: `https://yourwebsite.com/sitemap.xml`  
   *(Add the sitemap file first — see Step 5.)*

**Why:** Google will not reliably show your site in search until it knows the site exists and what pages you have.

---

#### Step 2: Do the same for Bing (optional but easy)

1. Go to [Bing Webmaster Tools](https://www.bing.com/webmasters)
2. Add your site and verify it (similar to Google)
3. Submit the same sitemap when it is ready

**Why:** Some people still use Bing; setup takes about 10 minutes.

---

#### Step 3: Create a LinkedIn company page

1. On LinkedIn, create a **Company Page** for R-ODEA
2. Fill in:
   - Company name: **R-ODEA**
   - Website: your real URL
   - Short description: what you do + London, Hong Kong, Melbourne
   - Logo: same as your website
3. Post occasionally (projects, sectors you work in, Asia market insights)

**Why:** When people Google "R-ODEA", LinkedIn often appears next to your website. It also sends visitors to your site.

---

### Part 2: Fix what is on each page

Every page needs **three things** Google reads:

| What | Where it is | Rule |
|------|-------------|------|
| **Page title** | Browser tab / Google blue link | Short, clear, include R-ODEA + what the page is about |
| **Meta description** | Text under the link in Google | 1–2 sentences: what you do, who you help, where you work |
| **Visible words on the page** | What visitors actually read | Real sentences — not hidden, not keyword lists |

---

#### Step 4: Update each page

See [Page-by-page copy to use](#page-by-page-copy-to-use) below for exact text to copy.

---

#### Step 5: Add sitemap.xml and robots.txt

Your site needs two small files:

- **`sitemap.xml`** — a list of all your pages for Google
- **`robots.txt`** — tells Google it can crawl your site and where the sitemap is

After these are live, go back to Step 1 and submit the sitemap in Google Search Console.

---

#### Step 6: Fix your images

On the homepage, some images have **empty** alt text (`alt=""`). Google and screen readers cannot understand those images.

**Rule:** Every image gets a short description.

| Image | Bad | Good |
|-------|-----|------|
| Peggy Gou | `alt=""` | `alt="Peggy Gou at The Edition Ginza — R-ODEA project"` |
| Charles Leclerc | `alt=""` | `alt="Charles Leclerc for Vogue China — R-ODEA consultancy for One Legacy"` |
| Emma Corrin | `alt=""` | `alt="Emma Corrin for The Standard London — R-ODEA"` |

**Why:** Helps accessibility and gives Google more context about your work.

---

#### Step 7: Add social sharing tags

When someone shares your site on LinkedIn or elsewhere, you want a nice title, description, and image — not a blank box.

This means adding Open Graph tags in the `<head>` of each page. These are invisible to visitors but control how links look when shared.

**Why:** Better-looking links get more clicks = more traffic.

---

#### Step 8: Do not hide keywords

**Do not:**

- Put white text on a white background
- Hide a list of keywords at the bottom of the page
- Stuff keywords in invisible divs

**Do:**

- Use normal sentences on the page
- Use good titles and descriptions (Step 4)
- Describe images properly (Step 6)

---

### Part 3: Grow traffic over time (no code required)

#### Step 9: Add a "Work" or "Selected projects" section

Even 3–5 short case studies help. For each one include:

- Client or project name (if you are allowed to say it)
- What you did (PR, partnership, launch, Asia entry)
- 2–3 sentences

**Why:** People search things like "luxury brand launch Hong Kong PR" — case studies help you show up for those searches.

---

#### Step 10: Write a few short articles a year (optional but powerful)

Example topics:

- How luxury brands enter the Asian market
- What mother agents should know about PR in China and Hong Kong
- Brand partnerships in fashion: what works in 2026

**Length:** 400–800 words is enough.  
**Where:** A simple "Insights" page on your site, or LinkedIn articles that link back to your site.

**Why:** This is how you start appearing for searches beyond just "R-ODEA".

---

#### Step 11: Get other websites to link to you

Easiest wins:

- LinkedIn company page → your website
- Instagram bio → your website
- Email signature → your website
- Any press, interviews, or client features → ask for a link to r-odea.com
- Industry directories (luxury, PR, consultancy lists)

**Why:** Google trusts sites that other reputable sites link to.

## What to do over time

### Quick wins (technical — on the website)

1. Add `sitemap.xml` and `robots.txt`
2. Improve titles and meta descriptions on every page
3. Add Open Graph tags for social sharing
4. Fix empty image alt attributes
5. Register in Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools

### Content wins (bigger long-term impact)

1. Add an H1 or subheading on the homepage that states what you do (keep the slogan as secondary copy)
2. Case studies or "Selected work" with real project names (where allowed)
3. A short insights/news section — even 4–6 articles per year helps
4. Location pages if you want local visibility (e.g. "Luxury consultancy Hong Kong")

### Off-site (often matters more than on-page SEO)

1. LinkedIn company page linked to the site
2. Listings on industry directories (luxury, PR, consultancy)
3. Press and client features that link back to you
4. Consistent business name and contact details everywhere online

## Quick reference: do's and don'ts

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| How findable am I now? | Easy for **brand name** searches; hard for **service/location** searches |
| Should I use keywords? | Yes — in titles, descriptions, headings, and real copy |
| Can I hide keywords? | **No** — use them visibly and naturally instead |
| What is the single most important fix? | Add one plain-English sentence on the homepage explaining what R-ODEA does |
| What is the best free tool? | Google Search Console |
| How long until I see results? | Branded searches improve quickly once Search Console is set up; competitive keywords take months of content and links |

## Summary

SEO is not about tricks or hidden text. It is about making it **obvious to Google and to humans** what R-ODEA does, who you help, and where you work.

**The three most important things:**

1. **Tell Google your site exists** (Search Console + sitemap)
2. **Say clearly what you do on every page** (titles, descriptions, visible copy)
3. **Build trust over time** (LinkedIn, case studies, articles, links from other sites)
